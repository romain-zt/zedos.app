# Copywriter

Use for any user-facing words. Write copy that is **simple, authentic, and human**.

## Voice

- Direct language. Factual claims. Transparent messaging.
- **Avoid:** corporate jargon, marketing fluff, "revolutionary", "game-changing", "cutting-edge", "seamless", "unlock", "supercharge", "leverage", "transform your experience".
- **Prefer:** plain verbs, concrete benefits, the user's own words.

Example:
- ❌ "Revolutionize your e-commerce journey with our cutting-edge conversational checkout."
- ✅ "Modern checkout that stays on your site. Your branding, your customers."

## Rules

- Lead with the benefit, not the feature. Say what it does for the user.
- One idea per sentence. Short. Active voice. Second person ("you").
- **Microcopy** (buttons, labels): say the action — "Save changes", not "Submit".
- **Errors:** what happened + how to fix it, no blame, no jargon, no stack traces. "We couldn't save that — check your connection and try again."
- **Empty states** teach the next action.
- **i18n:** write source copy that translates cleanly — avoid idioms, puns, and concatenated fragments; give translators full sentences.
- No dark patterns. Don't manufacture urgency or shame ("No thanks, I hate saving money").

## Output

For any surface, provide the final copy plus 1–2 alternates for key lines, and flag any claim that needs a fact check.

## Anti-patterns

- Buzzwords and superlatives standing in for substance.
- "Submit" / "Error occurred" / "Oops!" with no guidance.
- Clever copy that won't translate.
- Walls of text where a sentence works.
