## Working assumption

The current repository PRD describes an earlier product for solo founders creating PRDs. For this public landing page only, the 2026-07-11 wellness-platform brief supersedes that positioning. The existing signed-in product and legacy PRD are not being relabelled as wellness functionality.

Booking, payments, reminders, and operational concepts on the page must be labelled as early product direction until each workflow is validated and available. The page must never imply that an illustrative interface is a live customer screenshot.

## 1. Positioning statement

**Zedos is the ownable digital foundation for reservation-led wellness businesses: a branded website and booking experience that can launch simply, change quickly, and grow into custom software without another rebuild.**

Customer shorthand:

**A website and booking experience built to grow with your studio.**

## 2. Primary target audience

Lead with independent Pilates, yoga, and fitness studios, then include massage practices, wellness centres, and small multi-practitioner care businesses.

The highest-potential first customer:

- has 2–15 practitioners and one to three locations;
- depends on reservations rather than a large product catalogue;
- already uses a website plus a separate booking platform;
- cares about brand and the full customer journey;
- has a concrete change, launch, or migration in the next six months;
- expects needs that templates and plugins may not cover;
- will collaborate closely as an early design partner.

Solo practitioners remain welcome, but they are not the lead segment: many are adequately served by a simple site and Calendly. Large chains are also not the lead segment because they need proven enterprise operations before Zedos is ready.

## 3. Principal customer problem

The customer journey is split across tools that were never designed together. The owner manages the handoffs between the website, booking system, payments, reminders, plugins, and external developers. Routine changes are slow; distinctive changes are often impossible; switching later risks another rebuild.

The emotional problem is not “too many tools” alone. It is the loss of momentum and control when the business changes.

## 4. Core value proposition

Zedos gives a wellness business one flexible foundation for its website, booking journey, and future operational software. The owner can handle simple updates directly, request safe routine changes, or bring in a developer for complex work—while retaining control of the code and data.

## 5. Main differentiation

Zedos preserves optionality.

Closed builders optimise for the first launch. Booking SaaS products optimise for a standard workflow. Agencies optimise for bespoke delivery. Zedos is designed to combine a simple starting experience with an ownable, standard codebase that can keep evolving.

The retention promise is convenience and execution quality, not technical lock-in.

## 6. Messaging hierarchy

1. **Immediate value:** a better website and booking experience for a reservation-led wellness business.
2. **Recognition:** the current fragmented stack creates a fragmented customer journey.
3. **Daily experience:** edit directly, ask for a routine change, or route complex work to a person.
4. **Strategic freedom:** keep control of code, data, and developer choice.
5. **Long-term value:** evolve into member experiences, custom workflows, and deeper integrations without starting over.
6. **Trust:** private pilot, real founder involvement, honest capability boundaries, and Little Biceps as the founding pilot.
7. **Conversion:** apply for a founder-led platform review and pilot, not a generic mailing list.

Booking leads the narrative because it is the urgent, recognisable business job. Ownership appears after the reader understands the practical value. AI is supporting evidence, not the headline.

Do not mention credits on the pre-launch page. They add pricing complexity before the visitor understands the product. Complex human work is described as separately scoped and explicitly approved.

### K. FAQ

#### Is Zedos another AI website builder?

No. AI can help with routine work, but the result is a real, editable codebase and structured data—not a proprietary AI-generated page format. Zedos is designed to support manual editing, professional development, and long-term ownership.

#### What is available today?

Zedos is in private pilot. The landing-page interfaces describe the product direction, not a claim that every booking workflow is already live. Scope is agreed pilot by pilot, and we will say clearly what is ready, what can integrate with an existing tool, and what remains planned.

#### Can Zedos replace my current booking platform?

Potentially, but not by default on day one. We first map the booking rules your business relies on. An early pilot may keep the current platform connected while Zedos takes over the website and selected parts of the journey. We only recommend replacement when the required workflow is ready.

#### Will I own my website?

The intended model is clear: your business retains control of its project code and data. Ownership, access, export, infrastructure, and handover terms will be documented in the pilot agreement before work begins.

#### What happens if I leave?

You should be able to take over the project, move the infrastructure, or hand the standard codebase to another capable team. The exact handover process will be documented and tested before the no-lock-in promise is used as an unconditional contractual claim.

#### Can my own developer work on it?

Yes. Zedos uses standard technologies and Git-based workflows so an appropriately skilled developer or agency can extend the same project. Technical access is optional for the business owner, not a requirement for using Zedos.

#### Can AI break my production website?

AI is not given blanket permission to change important production workflows. Routine requests should be constrained and reviewable. Ambiguous, specialised, or high-risk work is routed to a person or deferred. The safety controls themselves must be demonstrated before stronger claims are made.

#### What happens when AI cannot do the work?

You receive a clear next path: approve separately scoped implementation, ask your own developer, handle it independently, or leave it for later. A failed AI attempt should never become forced paid work.

#### Is Zedos only a website?

It can start there, but the foundation is intended to support a richer booking journey, member experiences, custom operations, and integrations. The point is to evolve the same project instead of migrating to a new platform each time.

#### Do I need technical knowledge?

No. Everyday use is designed for a business owner. Code and infrastructure access exist to preserve freedom and enable advanced work; they are not required for routine operation.

#### How much will it cost?

Public pricing is not final. Pilot scope, ongoing platform costs, and any separately priced development work will be explained before commitment. Do not claim savings against an existing stack until real total-cost comparisons are available.

#### Why join now?

Early partners receive direct founder involvement, a review of their current setup, and meaningful influence over which booking workflows are built first. Joining the list is not a purchase commitment.

## 12. Visual direction

- Warm ivory canvas, deep botanical ink, muted sage, and a restrained clay accent.
- Editorial serif for major statements; clean sans-serif for controls and operational copy.
- Generous whitespace, fine rules, tactile cards, and moderate radii.
- Calm wellness softness balanced by precise technical grids and status labels.
- Interface concepts use real language, visible review states, and clear “illustrative” labels.
- No stock wellness photography in V0. Real pilot photography should replace concepts only when permission and a coherent art direction are available.
- No robots, neural imagery, excessive gradients, glass-heavy dashboards, or generic purple SaaS styling.

## 13. Interaction and motion direction

- Sticky, compact navigation with a visible early-access CTA.
- Accessible scenario tabs that change the request, execution route, and outcome.
- Two-step form with immediate persistence after step one.
- Native disclosure patterns for FAQ.
- Motion limited to 180–350 ms fades, small translations, and state changes.
- No scroll-jacking, autoplay video, parallax, or continuous decorative animation.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.

## 15. Mobile behaviour

- Base layout targets 320–375 px.
- Header keeps logo and a 44 px CTA; secondary navigation collapses.
- Hero CTA buttons stack full-width, then become inline above the small breakpoint.
- Mockups simplify decoration before shrinking readable content.
- Journey and scenario controls remain keyboard-operable and horizontally scroll only when unavoidable.
- All cards become a single readable column.
- Form fields use at least 16 px input text to avoid mobile zoom and at least 44 px controls.
- Check at 320, 375, 768, 1024, and 1440 px.

## 16. Accessibility

- One `h1`; section headings follow a logical hierarchy.
- Semantic header, navigation, main, sections, lists, form, and footer.
- Skip link and visible focus styles.
- WCAG AA colour contrast.
- Labels remain visible; placeholders are examples only.
- Form errors are field-specific and announced through live regions.
- Scenario controls expose tab roles, selected state, and relationships.
- Decorative mockups are hidden from assistive technology or have a concise explanatory label.
- Touch targets are at least 44 by 44 px.
- Reduced-motion users receive immediate state changes.

## 23. Why this page should convert

The page starts with the buyer’s job—website and booking—rather than architecture or AI. It names the operational fragmentation they already recognise, then introduces a credible alternative through realistic changes rather than a feature inventory. Ownership arrives as the answer to a practical fear: having to rebuild when the business evolves.

The page also makes being early feel deliberate rather than hidden. Honest comparison, visible concept labels, a real founding pilot, and founder-led review reduce the trust gap. The application flow captures a lead before asking for detailed qualification, while the offer filters for businesses with a real booking problem and a near-term reason to act.
